What is this, plainly?

Instawork Robotics Lab (we call it IRL) pays people to wear a small head-mounted camera (we send you the headset) while they do real work — in a restaurant, warehouse, store, hotel, and more. The video teaches AI and robots how everyday tasks actually get done.

You're not performing or acting. You just do your normal work with the headset camera running.

$20 / hour

Submit your video, we run a quick quality check, and $20 for every hour of usable footage lands in your account — in days, not weeks.

How recording works

  • You record short videos — 2 minutes minimum, 20 minutes maximum each.
  • When a video hits 20 minutes, you simply start a fresh one and keep going.
  • You start and stop recording yourself. You're always in control of the camera.
  • You only record at a workplace after a manager there has said yes.
  • Active physical work only — cooking, stocking, cleaning, packing, building, handling materials. Videos must show hands-on task work to qualify for payment.
  • Driving, sitting, standing idle, or watching others work does not qualify — the camera needs to capture you actively doing a task.
  • Submit your video in the app — once it clears a quick quality check, payment lands in your account.

Quick answers

Do I need experience?

No. If you can do the work, you can do this. The app walks you through recording.

When do I get paid?

After your video clears a quick quality review — in days, not weeks. You earn $20 for every hour of usable footage. Videos must show active hands-on work to qualify — driving or idle time does not count toward your hour.

What do I record?

Your normal work — cooking, stocking, cleaning, packing, or any hands-on task. After a manager okays it, you start the camera and go about your shift.

Is this a regular job?

You work as an independent contractor and choose when you record. There's no set schedule.
